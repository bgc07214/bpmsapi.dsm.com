Yemen’s Houthi rebels have attacked a British oil tanker in the Gulf of Aden. After Friday’s attack, the Marlin Luanda caught fire on its starboard side. The fire on the ship is still burning on Saturday. News from NDTV<\/p>\n

Quoting the Indian Navy, the media report said that the ship damaged by the Houthi attack had 22 Indians and a Bangladeshi crew on board. Their guided missile destroyer INS Visakhapatnam quickly responded after receiving a plea for help from the ship. Now this warship is trying to control the oil tanker fire.<\/p>\n

In a statement, the Indian Navy said its forces are always committed to maintaining smooth sailing at sea.<\/p>\n
