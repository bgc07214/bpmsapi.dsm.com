After Babri Masjid, there was a Hindu temple at the site of Gnanabapi Masjid in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India, according to the Archeology Department of the country. The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) has conducted a survey on this. As a result of the survey, it is claimed that there was a temple there before the construction of the mosque. The mosque was built in 1669 by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. BBC news<\/p>\n

In the middle of last year, the Varanasi District Court ordered the ASI to conduct a survey of the mosque premises. Based on four months of survey, scientific observations, study of architectural features, patterns, inscriptions, art and sculptures, it is easy to say that a Hindu temple existed there before the construction of the present structure, the published survey report said.<\/p>\n

Anupam Dwivedi, lawyer for plaintiff Rakhi Singh in the case, termed the survey as very important. He said, ‘This report will strengthen our case. It is very important for the cases in terms of evidence.’<\/p>\n

However, Muslims have informed that they received a copy of the ASI report last Thursday night. SM Yasin, joint secretary of Anjuman Intezamiya Mosque, who is in charge of managing the Gnanabapi Masjid, said, “This is a report, not a judgment.” The report is of 839 pages. It will take time to survey and analyze. Lawyers and experts will be consulted. Then it will be taken to the court for consideration.’<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
